hom·o·nym

noun

each of two or more words having the same spelling or pronunciation whilst having different meanings and origins


In choosing homonym as the theme for the second issue of Vernacular, we want to provide an idea that encourages creative exploration. Once you look past the technical definition, adjacent paths begin to come into focus. Roads you are now free to mozy, roam, or run. A homonym can be something surprising, misunderstood while still congruent. A singular expression equally belonging in multiple contexts. Camouflage, trompe d'œil, overlapping meanings, polymorphic identities.

Poems, lyrics, or short stories with wordplay, double entendres, various languages? A figure that appears to be one type of object or medium but is, in reality, another? Despite the look, feel, or sound the submissions in this issue should, in some way, be open to interpretation, provide illusion, delusion, or mirage.



Deadline: midnight on Pi Day (3.14.2020)






Submission Guidelines

  1. All pieces must be original work by the artist submitting. Multiple submissions are welcome, as are pieces under consideration by other publications.
  2. Artists can submit multiple mediums, through separate submission forms.
  3. Feel free to submit up to five images of visual work, in high-resolution .pdf or .jpeg.
  4. For writing submissions, please denote genre (fiction, nonfiction, poetry, flash fiction, script, or none). They should be less than 5,000 words, with no word count minimum. One piece of longform per submission, and up to three poems.
  5. Video submissions ought to be .mov or .mp4 files, up to three per submission. Please specify the medium you are submitting (dance, short film, performance, etc), as well as information on the videographer and the performers, if any, in the video.
  6. Please submit up to three audio files, in .mp3 format, specifying the medium you are using (vocals, instrument). Each file should have a cover photo attached, which will be used as the visual component of the Vimeo link upon publication.
  7. We encourage interdisciplinary work, and genre-melding. All genre delineations are to speed up the editing process.
